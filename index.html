Welcome to Latvia

Enjoy culture, entertainment and much more activities in Latvia, one of the amazing Baltic States.

What could you see and do in Latvia?

House of the BlackHeads (Rīga)

Must to see if you love architecture, also a museum now. The Old Town Rīga is the oldest part of our capital city, it is a city center, as well as a favorite place for recreation and entertainment for tourists.

Art Nouveau House Element (Rīga)

The Art Nouveau architecture in Riga makes up roughly one third of all the buildings in the centre of Riga, making Latvia's capital the city with the highest concentration of Art Nouveau architecture anywhere in the world.

Rundāle Castle (Bauska Municipality)

Want to enjoy some more masterpieces outside Riga? You shall definitely visit one of the two major baroque palaces in Latvia then. Located 67 km (42 miles) along the road A7 to Bauska.

Latvian National Opera (Rīga)

During the season (September-May), an average of six new performances are staged, maintaining a balance between the opera and ballet genres. In addition, more than 200 performances and several symphonic and chamber music concerts are shown in one season.

Freedom Monument (Rīga)

Nicknamed 'Milda', after a Baltic goddess of love, courtship, friendship and freedom, the monument is considered an important symbol of the freedom, independence, and sovereignty of Latvia and her people. The Guard of Honour is a beautiful ceremony to watch to feel alonside with Latvian patriots.

Dome Cathedral (Rīga)

Riga Cathedral has won the heart of Old Riga since July 25, 1211. Nowadays, a rich cultural life takes place there - concerts of local and foreign artists and various art exhibitions are held. Riga Cathedral organ is considered to be one of the world's most valuable historic organs.

Kronvalda Park (Rīga)

Latvia is a very green country with a lot of forests and parks. You can enjoy the nature not only in the countryside but also in all Latvian towns, Rīga included. If you are a flower lover, I highly recommend to visit The Botanical Garden of the University of Latvia.

Sigulda

Sigulda, located 53 km (33 miles) from the capital city Riga, is especially fascinating in autumn. Famous not only for its national park but also for its castles and bobsleigh, luge, and skeleton tracks

Jūrmala

And definitely you shall visit Jūrmala, ('seaside' in English), my hometown, which is about 25 km (16 miles) west of Riga. It is a resort town stretching 32 km (20 miles) and sandwiched between the Gulf of Riga and the Lielupe River.
